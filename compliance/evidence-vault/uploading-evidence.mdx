---
title: "Uploading Evidence"
description: "How to upload a document to the Evidence Vault, set expiry dates, and manage your files."
---

Uploading a document to the Evidence Vault takes less than a minute. You can add as much or as little detail as you need — at minimum just a name, category, and the file itself.

## Uploading a document

From the Evidence Vault, click **Upload Document**. Fill in the following:

**Name** — a clear, descriptive name for the document (e.g. "Public Liability Insurance 2025–2026", "Building WOF — Main Centre").

**Category** — select from the available categories, or choose a custom category you've created. If nothing fits, use **Other**.

**Scope** — choose whether this document applies to a specific centre or is **Business-wide** (covers your whole organisation).

**File** — upload a PDF, Word document, image, or other common document format.

Then optionally add:

**Issue date** — when the document was issued or came into effect.

**Expiry date** — when the document expires or is next due for renewal. Setting this enables expiry tracking and dashboard alerts.

**Reference number** — a licence number, policy number, or other reference from the issuing body.

**Notes** — any additional context useful to your team.

Click **Save** to upload the document to the Vault.

## Editing a document

To update a document's details — for example, to upload a renewed certificate and update the expiry date — open the document from the Vault list and click **Edit**. You can replace the file and update any of the fields. The previous file is replaced; the Vault doesn't keep version history on documents.

## Deleting a document

Open the document and click **Delete**. Deletion is permanent — the file is removed from storage. If you're replacing a document rather than removing it, use Edit instead.

## Searching and filtering

The Vault list can be filtered by category, expiry status (expired / expiring soon / current / no expiry), and scope (specific centre or business-wide). You can also search by document name or reference number.

## Expiry alerts

If you've set an expiry date, the document will show a colour-coded expiry badge in the list. Documents expiring within 30 days show amber; expired documents show red. Both are surfaced on the dashboard so your team is alerted before a document lapses.

When you renew a document, upload the new file and update the expiry date — this clears the alert automatically.

<Note>
  The Vault stores documents securely in private storage — files are not publicly accessible. Only users with Evidence Vault access for your business or centre can view them.
</Note>
