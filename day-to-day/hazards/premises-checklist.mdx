---
title: "Premises Checklist"
description: "How to set up your daily hazard check template and complete checks as a team."
---

The Premises Checklist is a configurable daily walkthrough tool for your centre. Your team works through a set of items each day, marking each one as a pass or fail. It's a simple, consistent way to catch hazards early — and any failed item can be escalated straight into your hazard register.

## Who can do what

Compliance users set up and manage the checklist template. Any staff member with access to the Hazard & Premises module can complete a daily check — you don't need compliance access to do the walkthrough.

## Setting up your template

Before your team can complete daily checks, a compliance user needs to configure the template. Go to the **Daily Checks** tab in the Hazard & Premises module and open the template settings.

For each item on your checklist, you can set:

- **Label** — what the item is checking (e.g. "Outdoor gate latches secure", "No trip hazards in main room")
- **Premises location** — optionally link the item to a specific area of your centre

Items can be reordered and activated or deactivated at any time. Only active items appear when staff complete a check.

<Note>
  There's one active template per centre. If your centre has multiple distinct areas, use the premises location field on each item to organise the checklist rather than creating multiple templates.
</Note>

## Completing a daily check

From the **Daily Checks** tab, click **Start Check** to begin. The active template loads with all items listed.

For each item:

- Mark it **Pass** or **Fail**
- Add optional notes — particularly useful if something needs follow-up even if it passes

Once all items are marked, add any overall notes for the check, then click **Submit**. This creates a dated record of the check, logged against your name.

## When an item fails

If an item is marked as fail, you have the option to **promote it to a hazard** in the standing register. This pre-fills the hazard title and premises location from the check item so you don't have to re-enter the details. A compliance user can then complete the full hazard record — adding control measures, risk ratings, and an owner.

The link between the daily check finding and the resulting hazard is recorded automatically, so the trail is clear.

## Check history

Past daily check records are listed in the **Daily Checks** tab with the date, who completed the check, and a summary of how many items passed and failed. You can open any past record to see the full item-by-item results and notes.

The dashboard flags if no daily check has been recorded today, so it's easy to see at a glance whether the walkthrough has been done.
