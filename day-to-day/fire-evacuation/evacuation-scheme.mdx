---
title: "Evacuation Scheme"
description: "How to record and maintain your FENZ-approved fire evacuation scheme."
---

Your fire evacuation scheme is the document approved by Fire and Emergency New Zealand (FENZ) that sets out how your centre will evacuate in the event of a fire. Every NZ early childhood centre must have a current, approved scheme.

## Scheme status

Your scheme will show one of five statuses:

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| 🟢 **Approved — Current** | Your scheme is FENZ-approved and up to date |
| 🟡 **In Progress** | Application submitted, awaiting FENZ approval |
| 🟡 **Unverified** | A scheme exists but FENZ records are unclear — needs confirmation |
| 🔴 **Approved — Expired** | Your scheme was previously approved but has since expired |
| 🔴 **No Scheme** | Your centre has no scheme on record — this is non-compliant |

A centre with no scheme or an expired scheme is non-compliant. This is highlighted on the module dashboard and your main dashboard until it's resolved.

## What the scheme record captures

- **FENZ reference number** — the reference assigned by FENZ
- **Date approved**
- **Normal occupants** — the number of occupants the scheme is designed for under normal conditions
- **Maximum occupants**
- **Submission details** — whether the scheme was submitted by your centre or through an external agency, who submitted it, when, and how
- **Documents** — upload up to five documents: the scheme document, site map, fire action notice, training plan, and any supporting files

## FENZ correspondence

Any communication with FENZ about your scheme is recorded in the correspondence log. Each entry captures:

- **Interaction date**
- **Interaction type** — e.g. email received, phone call, site visit (types are managed by the CCNZ team)
- **Comments** — what was discussed or decided
- **Attachments** — upload any letters, emails, or documents from FENZ
- **Action items** — any follow-up tasks that came out of the interaction, each with an assignee and due date

The correspondence log gives you a complete audit trail of every exchange with FENZ, which is useful at ERO review time.

## When your scheme expires

When you need to renew your scheme, create a new scheme record. The previous scheme is automatically archived — it stays on record but is no longer shown as your active scheme. Archived versions are accessible from the scheme page if you need them for reference.

## Scheme without approval yet

If you're in the process of getting your scheme approved, set the status to **In Progress** and record the submission details. Update the status to **Approved — Current** once FENZ confirms approval and you have a reference number.
