description: "How to work through the recruitment stages — interviews, references, safety checks, and offers."
---

The candidate detail page gives you a structured place to work through each recruitment stage. Sections don't lock based on the current stage — you can add an interview record, a reference check, or a police vetting record in any order, as information becomes available.

## Interviews

From the **Interviews** tab, click **Add Interview** to record an interview. Each interview record captures:

- Date, time, and method
- Who conducted the interview
- Questions and responses — select from the question library or add ad-hoc questions on the fly. Questions added ad-hoc are saved to your library for future use.
- A rating for each response
- Overall recommendation — Progress, Hold, or Decline

You can record multiple interviews for the same candidate.

## Reference checks

From the **References** tab, click **Add Reference Check**. Referee details are pre-filled from the candidate's employment history — select which referee you're checking, or add one manually if they're not in the application.

Each reference check records:

- Date and method of contact
- Questions and responses (same question library as interviews)
- Overall summary
- Recommendation — Positive, Neutral, Concerns, or Do Not Hire

## Safety check

The **Safety Check** tab has two sections:

**Identity verification** — a checklist of identity documents. Tick each document type as it's sighted and optionally upload a copy. Standard types include passport, driver's licence, birth certificate, visa, and others.

**Police vetting** — add one or more vetting records. Each record captures the vetting agency, form signed date, submission date, result, and next due date (auto-calculated as three years from the result date). Police vetting records are carried across to the staff profile on conversion.

## Offer management

From the **Offer** tab, click **Add Offer** to record the job offer details:

- Date and method of offer
- Role, centre, start date, and employment type
- Outcome — Pending, Accepted, Declined, or Withdrawn

When outcome is set to **Accepted**, the candidate's stage automatically advances to Offer Accepted and the Convert to Staff Member button becomes available.

## Declining a candidate

To decline a candidate at any stage, click **Decline** from the Overview tab. Select a reason (internal only — never shared with the candidate) and add an optional internal note. You can optionally send a generic, kind rejection email to the candidate — the reason is never included in the email.

Declined candidates are hidden from the default candidates list. Use the **Show declined** toggle to include them.

## Activity timeline

Every action on a candidate record is logged automatically to the **Activity** tab — status changes, emails sent, interviews added, reference checks recorded, notes, and more. The timeline gives you a complete audit trail of the recruitment process without any extra work.
