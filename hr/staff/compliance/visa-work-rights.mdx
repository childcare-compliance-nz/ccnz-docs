---
title: "Visa & Work Rights"
description: "How to record and track visa and work rights status for staff members."
---

All staff members must be legally entitled to work in New Zealand. The Visa & Work Rights section on the Compliance tab records their work rights status and tracks any visa expiry dates, with a full history of every update.

## Recording visa and work rights

From the staff profile **Compliance** tab, scroll to the **Visa / Work Rights** section.

Fill in:

- **Entitled to work in NZ** — Yes or No
- **Visa type** (if applicable):
  - NZ Citizen
  - NZ Permanent Resident
  - Work Visa
  - Other
- **Visa expiry date** — required for work visas; not needed for NZ citizens and permanent residents

Click **Save**. A new entry is added to the history, and the status badge updates automatically.

## Work rights status

The status badge is calculated from the visa expiry date (where applicable):

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| 🟢 **Current** | Valid and not due to expire soon |
| 🟡 **Expiring Soon** | Visa expires within 30 days |
| 🔴 **Expired** | Visa expiry date has passed |

NZ citizens and permanent residents show as Current without an expiry date.

## Visa and work rights history

Every time visa or work rights details are saved, a new row is added to the Visa / Work Rights History table below the form. The history shows:

- Entitlement status
- Visa type
- Visa expiry date
- Status badge
- Who recorded it and when

History is permanent — entries cannot be edited or deleted. This gives you an auditable record of work rights status over time, which is important for both compliance and employment law purposes.

## Staff from overseas

For staff on work visas, keep the expiry date up to date as their visa is renewed. When a new visa is granted, save the updated details — the new record becomes current and the previous one moves to history.
